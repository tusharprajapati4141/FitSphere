11 Basic Calisthenics Exercises: Types, Importance, and Risks

Shweta Kumari
April 8, 2024

Calisthenics might be the word your ancestors used for physical exercise. The development of calisthenics dates back to ancient Greece, and it gained popularity once more in the early 1800s. Interestingly, in the twenty-first century, it is now seeing a significant resurgence. Though there are many different kinds of exercises, calisthenics might be the best. Since the workout primarily uses body weight, it is fairly simple and convenient to do. Repetition and increasing your body’s endurance are vital components. Instead of using additional equipment, these low-resistance exercises use the body weight.

For those who prefer a different kind of exercise, calisthenics offers both a warm-up and cool-down. Today, these exercises are a popular option among health-conscious individuals, law enforcement officers, athletes in fitness training, and anyone attempting to stay in shape as a warm-up or body-building activity.


Types of Calisthenics Exercises

Calisthenics exercises are a type of resistance training that basically uses one’s body weight with minimal or no equipment. It originated in Ancient Greece and was designed to be quick and efficient, engaging multiple large muscle groups with each exercise. It simply incorporates the repetition of movements using muscle groups at a moderate pace. People only need their body weight to do Calisthenics. It has many health benefits, such as improving body posture, weight management, fitness level, flexibility and strength.

Calisthenics exercises are like a buffet of fitness, offering a variety that suits everyone’s taste. Whether someone’s a beginner or a fitness enthusiast, Calisthenics has something for everyone

Here are the six different types of Calisthenics exercises that can transform the body and fitness levels!

Freestyle Calisthenics

Freestyle Calisthenics involves exciting moves like bar transfers and clapping dips. While it is fun, it is crucial to get professional coaching to prevent injuries, especially for beginners.

Aesthetic Calisthenics

Aesthetics Calisthenics is the secret weapon of bodybuilders for those muscles. It combines basic movements, gradually intensifying body strength and muscle. It is a popular choice for those lifting weights.

Street Workout Calisthenics

A workout for anywhere! Street workout Calisthenics lets people flaunt their skills in public areas. From handstands to one-arm push-ups, it is all about showcasing strength creatively.

Flexible Calisthenics

Professionals in flexible Calisthenics can hold positions for minutes, focusing on balance and joint alignment. It is ideal for injury recovery and enhancing endurance.

Summary

All one needs for calisthenics is one’s weight. They are a resistance exercise that primarily makes use of the user’s body weight and little to no equipment. It basically consists of moderately paced muscle group repetitions of movements. The number of calories burned during Calisthenics depends on factors like weight and age. On average, 30 minutes of Calisthenics can burn between 135 to 355 calories. Calisthenics offers a variety of exercises catering to diverse preferences and fitness levels. From Freestyle Calisthenics, focusing on exciting moves like bar transfers, to Military Calisthenics resembling boot camp training, and Static Calisthenics, emphasising holding positions, each type caters to specific goals and interests.


Advantages of Calisthenics Exercises

Here are some of the reasons why people need to incorporate calisthenics exercises in their fitness routine:

Enhances Self-Confidence

Regular Calisthenics boosts confidence, shaping body and mind positively and making people feel healthier and more confident.

Convenient and Versatile

Calisthenics requires no fancy equipment. People can work out anywhere, anytime, using their body weight.

Beginner-Friendly

Calisthenics exercises like body-weight squats and push-ups are perfect for beginners. Master the basics, then progress to more challenging variations.

Easy to Modify

Modify the Calisthenics routine to match people’s fitness levels. Small changes, like using a resistance band, can make exercises more challenging.

Pair with Other Workouts

Combine Calisthenics with cardio or other training styles for a well-rounded fitness routine.

Summary

Calisthenics offers several benefits, including enhancing self-confidence, being convenient and versatile, beginner-friendly, easy to modify, and suitable for pairing with other workouts. It’s an accessible way to achieve a well-rounded fitness routine.


Health Benefits of Calisthenics

Calisthenics isn’t just about getting fit; it offers a plethora of health benefits. People can perform multiple repetitions of every exercise to help increase muscle mass and extend endurance. Here are the key advantages:

Boosts the Body’s Strength and Endurance

People can become stronger by utilising their body weight through calisthenics, which increases muscle endurance, strength, and holding power. When people perform calisthenics, it leads to stress on parts of their muscles and muscle groups, which results in hypertrophy. They also sustain microscopic injury to their muscle tissue, which gets replaced by new muscle tissue. People build new muscle fibre into the small gaps in their muscles during the recovery time in between workouts, making their muscles bigger and stronger. Calisthenics, in contrast to bodybuilding and weightlifting, focuses on boosting stamina as well.

Improves Flexibility and Mobility

Calisthenics is a movement-oriented sport that improves posture, balance, and flexibility, preventing locked up or shortened muscles. Stretching muscles during routines enhances flexibility, while the need to balance body weight hones a sense of balance. Improved posture and muscles become more relaxed as the body strengthens muscles, reducing the risk of back and joint aches.

Improves Mental Health

Regular exercise, including calisthenics, positively impacts mental well-being. It boosts confidence, releases endorphins, and promotes a happier state of mind. Moreover, during pre and post-workout, the body releases endorphins, a hormone that makes people feel comfortable and calm.

Fights and Prevents Obesity

Obesity is the primary concern for most diseases like cardiovascular issues, hypertension, breathing problems, and more. Calisthenics can help people shed excess weight and lower cholesterol. Beginners can start with basic exercises such as lunges, squats, and push-ups and then gradually move to intense ones. It is the best way to combat obesity and its related issues. However, consult the doctor or registered dietician to avoid any health injuries.

Regulates Blood Sugar

An unhealthy lifestyle results in obesity, which can further lead to diabetes. Performing Calisthenics exercises can help people minimise the risk of having diabetes and manage blood sugar levels by promoting weight loss. It increases muscle capacity to absorb glucose. It maintains blood sugar at an average level, reducing the risk of diabetes.

Lowers the Risk of Heart Disease

Calisthenics can lower blood sugar levels, contributing to reduced heart disease. It manages high cholesterol and high blood pressure by keeping the body active. Combine calisthenics with a balanced diet and lifestyle choices to reap the maximum benefits of it.

Helps Develop a Healthy Physique

Incorporating Calisthenics helps sculpt the body, making it lean and muscular. It helps in achieving a healthy physique with well-defined muscles and toned abs.

Summary

Calisthenics is a technique that helps people get stronger by using their body weight to develop their muscle endurance, strength, and holding power. Unlike weightlifting and bodybuilding, calisthenics also emphasises increasing stamina. A movement-based sport, calisthenics helps avoid shortened or locked muscles by enhancing flexibility, balance, and posture. In addition, the body releases endorphins—a hormone that promotes comfort and calmness—both before and after this exercise. By encouraging weight loss, calisthenics exercise can help people regulate their blood sugar levels and reduce their chance of developing diabetes. To get the most out of calisthenics, combine it with a healthy diet and way of life.


Precautions and Risks

Before diving into Calisthenics, it is crucial to be aware of potential risks and take necessary precautions:

  • Consult the doctor when people are on medications or pregnant before incorporating any new fitness regime.

  • Monitor heart rate during and after workout. Aim for 64-76% of the maximum heart rate for moderate intensity.

  • Always warm up and stretch before the Calisthenics session to prevent injuries.

  • Start with basic, low-intensity exercises and progress gradually to advanced ones.

  • Take regular breaks during intense routines, around 10 minutes, to avoid injuries and overexertion.

  • Know your body and allow it for proper recovery after intense Calisthenic workouts.

  • Stay hydrated throughout the calisthenic sessions.

Summary

Before starting a Calisthenics routine, it’s essential to consult a doctor, monitor heart rate, warm up and stretch, progress gradually, take breaks, and allow proper recovery to prevent injuries. For moderate intensity, aim for 64–76% of the maximum heart rate. To avoid injuries, warm up and stretch before any calisthenics exercise.

Conclusion

Calisthenics are exercises that don’t rely on anything but body weight. Calisthenics exercises build strength and improve body posture and are more similar to weight-based training exercises. If someone is interested in incorporating Calisthenics workouts into their exercise plan, it is essential to start small and focus on consistency. They also should set attainable goals, prioritise their workouts, and ensure they are using the proper form. If someone needs help with how to make a plan or how to do certain moves, work with a personal trainer.
